Assessing AI-Generated Fashion Images-An Evaluation Framework for Education-

Poster presentation at 2025학년 한국의류학회 추계학술대회

Abstract

Generative AI provides beginner fashion designers with an accessible way to explore design ideas, but its educational value depends on how accurately generated images reflect a learner’s intended design choices. This research proposes a multi-metric evaluation framework to assess both the technical reliability and creative usability of AI-generated fashion images in a learning context. The framework combines three complementary components: a checklist-based qualitative assessment of key design elements (garment type, silhouette, style, color, pattern, and material), CLIP-based metrics to evaluate semantic alignment between prompts and outputs, and an aesthetic predictor to estimate visual quality and human-like appeal. A sample case study demonstrates how these metrics jointly reveal strengths and limitations in generated results. By integrating qualitative structure with quantitative and aesthetic evaluation, the proposed framework offers a balanced approach to verifying image accuracy while maintaining visual appeal. This method helps reduce learner confusion and supports the use of generative AI as a trustworthy and effective tool in fashion design education.
