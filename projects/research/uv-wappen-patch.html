Development of a Detachable UV Monitoring Wappen Patch: Real-Time Sun Exposure Management

Poster presentation at ITMC2024 Conference & Sustainable Smart Fiber Exhibition

Abstract

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is essential for vitamin D synthesis but poses serious health risks when exposure is excessive, including skin damage and increased cancer risk. As outdoor activities increase, there is a growing need for practical and user-friendly solutions to monitor UV exposure in daily life. This study presents a detachable, wearable UV monitoring wappen patch designed to seamlessly integrate into everyday clothing and accessories. The system combines a low-power UV photodiode sensor with Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) communication, a compact microcontroller, and a rechargeable lithium-polymer battery to provide real-time UV index monitoring via a mobile application. UV exposure data are classified into five risk levels, triggering personalized notifications and actionable health guidance. The patch was tested under diverse real-world conditions and attachment scenarios, demonstrating stable performance, durability, and ease of use. By minimizing user intervention and integrating naturally into daily wear, the proposed UV monitoring patch offers an effective approach to personalized sun exposure management. This research highlights the potential of wearable health technologies to support preventive care and reduce UV-related health risks through seamless, user-centered design.
