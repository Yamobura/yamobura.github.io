AI-Driven Approaches to Enhancing Accessibility of Luxury Fashion Websites

Poster presentation at ICES 2025 (The 7th International Conference of Emotion and Sensibility)

Abstract

Web accessibility remains a major challenge despite the global expansion of digital commerce. Around 15% of the world’s population lives with disabilities, yet nearly 96% of web homepages still fail to meet Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). This study examines accessibility in the luxury fashion sector by evaluating seven global brand websites using automated WCAG 2.2 compliance analysis. The findings reveal recurring barriers, including missing alternative text, insufficient color contrast, incomplete ARIA structures, unclear link labels, and inaccessible video content. These issues reflect deeper structural problems, such as the low prioritization of accessibility in design workflows and implicit age bias in digital fashion platforms. The study argues that accessibility should be understood not only as legal compliance, but as a strategic and ethical opportunity. It highlights the potential of AI-driven tools—such as automated alt-text and caption generation—to support inclusive design without compromising visual identity, positioning accessibility as a driver of innovation in digital fashion.
